# Presentation Builder Playbooks

**What:** Writer playbooks that build presentations, including decks in the Xfinity template.

**Status:** Working at a functional level, but quality depends on better upfront story-shaping.

**Key learning:** A presentation builder should not jump straight from transcript/source material to slides. It needs preliminary steps that translate raw information into story, content blocks, goals, and slide-level direction.

**Samson's pattern:** For John G.'s presentation builder, Samson designed seven interview questions before the playbook builds the deck. The last question is roughly "is there anything else I should know?" TODO: capture the exact seven questions when available.

**Operating principle:** Treat the playbook like a new content strategist joining the team. The agent needs the same answers a human teammate would need: what story to tell, who is in the room, what can be tabled, what needs action, and how the material should be framed.

**Human judgment boundary:** LLMs can emulate examples and patterns, but judgment is forward-looking. The human still has to provide the desired goal, framing, and instructions for navigating the messy middle.
