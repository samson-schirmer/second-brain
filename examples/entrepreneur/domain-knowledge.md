## Industry Context

B2B brand strategy is undergoing a huge shift right now. The old model — hire an agency for $100K+ for a 3-month engagement — is being compressed by solo practitioners with AI tools who can deliver similar quality in weeks for a fraction of the cost. Most agencies haven't adapted to this. Meanwhile, founders are increasingly savvy about branding and messaging, so they want a strategic partner, not a vendor who presents three logo options. The best clients are founders who know they have a messaging problem and want someone opinionated to solve it — not founders who want a committee process.

## Frameworks and Mental Models

"What, for whom, so that" — my core positioning formula. Every positioning statement answers: what is it, who is it for, and what changes for them. April Dunford's category design thinking (Obviously Awesome) — I use her methodology heavily. StoryBrand for narrative structure when it fits, though I think it's overused. "The Only Test" — can you say "we are the only [category] that [differentiator]"? If you can't, your positioning isn't sharp enough.
