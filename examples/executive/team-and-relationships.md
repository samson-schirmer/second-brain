# Team and Relationships

## David Nakamura

**Role:** CEO
**Relationship:** My boss.
**How We Interact:** Weekly exec staff meeting, biweekly 1:1, Slack for urgent items.
**What He Needs From Me:** Confidence that engineering can deliver what the business needs. Early warning on risks — technical, timeline, or people. Clear communication about tradeoffs when product asks exceed engineering capacity.
**What I Need From Him:** Headcount approvals, air cover for infrastructure investments that don't have immediate product ROI, alignment on priorities when product and engineering roadmaps conflict.
**Context for Agents:** David is a former engineer who became a business operator. He appreciates technical depth but wants it translated into business impact. Don't use jargon without connecting it to outcomes. He values brevity — if it takes more than five minutes to explain, write a doc.

## Lisa Fernandez

**Role:** CPO
**Relationship:** Peer — my product counterpart.
**How We Interact:** Weekly cross-functional sync, informal Slack conversations daily, quarterly planning deep dives.
**What She Needs From Me:** Realistic timelines, clear communication about technical constraints, willingness to find creative solutions when timelines are tight.
**What I Need From Her:** Stable priorities, well-defined requirements before engineering starts, early visibility into upcoming product asks so I can plan capacity.
**Context for Agents:** Lisa and I have a good relationship but a structural tension — she always wants more shipped faster, I need to protect quality and team sustainability. We've learned to be direct with each other about it. If prepping me for a conversation with her, frame things in terms of product outcomes, not engineering effort.

## Runa Johansson

**Role:** AI/ML Engineering Lead (new hire)
**Relationship:** Direct report — newest of my leads.
**How We Interact:** Weekly 1:1, frequent Slack check-ins (she's ramping up and needs more access to me right now).
**What She Needs From Me:** Clear charter, political support for the AI team's existence (some leads see it as a resource grab), introductions across the org.
**What I Need From Her:** A realistic first-project proposal, honest assessment of what we can do with a small team vs. what needs more headcount.
**Context for Agents:** Runa is brilliant and opinionated. She came from a much larger ML team at a big tech company and is adjusting to startup pace. I need to give her room to operate while making sure she calibrates to our scale.

## Derek Huang

**Role:** Platform Engineering Lead
**Relationship:** Direct report — most senior of my leads, been here since early days.
**How We Interact:** Biweekly 1:1, architecture reviews, incident response (he's usually first on the scene).
**What He Needs From Me:** Trust and autonomy. Clear priority calls when his team is overloaded. Public recognition — he's the backbone of the org and doesn't always get visibility.
**What I Need From Him:** The migration to succeed without incidents. Mentorship of junior engineers on his team. Honest pushback when I'm underestimating complexity.
**Context for Agents:** Derek communicates through code and architecture diagrams more than words. If preparing something for a meeting with him, include a system diagram or data flow — he thinks spatially.

## Kenji Watanabe

**Role:** DevOps/Infrastructure Lead
**Relationship:** Direct report.
**How We Interact:** Biweekly 1:1, incident retrospectives, reliability review.
**What He Needs From Me:** Budget for tooling, headcount (his team is stretched thinnest), and executive support for enforcing on-call standards across teams.
**What I Need From Him:** Platform stability, observability improvements, and the operational rigor that keeps us from having another bad quarter.
**Context for Agents:** Kenji is quiet in meetings but extremely thorough in writing. Ask him for a written assessment and you'll get something excellent. Put him on the spot verbally and you'll get a shorter, less complete answer.
