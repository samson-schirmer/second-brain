# Preferences and Constraints

## Hard Constraints

Pacific time. I don't take meetings before 9am or after 5:30pm PT. Fridays are heads-down writing time — I decline meetings on Fridays unless it's genuinely urgent. I have a hard stop at 5pm on Tuesdays and Thursdays for childcare pickup.

## Strong Preferences

Specs live in Google Docs, not Notion. I know the team wiki is in Notion, but specs need the commenting and suggestion features that Docs does better. All specs follow the standard format — Problem, Proposed Solution, Success Metrics, Open Questions. No exceptions.

I want to read a doc before a meeting about that doc. Don't schedule a meeting to "walk me through" a spec — send it first, give me a day, then let's talk about the open questions.

## Things I Hate

Meetings without agendas. Status meetings that could be a Loom or a Slack post. Being CC'd on email threads where I don't have an action item. Specs that describe a solution without stating the problem first. The phrase "can we hop on a quick call?" — just ask your question in Slack.

## Personal Constraints

I have two kids (ages 3 and 6). The Tuesday/Thursday hard stops are non-negotiable. I occasionally need to handle a sick kid situation on short notice — when that happens I shift to async for the day but stay responsive on Slack.

## AI Output Preferences

Keep things concise — I'd rather get a tight half-page than a thorough two pages. Use bullet points for anything with multiple items. Don't use headers for short responses — just write. For spec drafts, match our format exactly (Problem, Proposed Solution, Success Metrics, Open Questions). Never use corporate language — write like a human.
